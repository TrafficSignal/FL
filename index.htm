Exception List and Traffic Signal Maintaining Agencies (TSMA) in Florida

    Exceptions:
  • Only Traffic Signal Maintaining Agencies that have more than 20 interconnected signals and/or more than 20 traffic signals in the State Highway System (SHS) have been mapped by TRINT. Fifty seven TSMA are being mapped by TRINT (49 complete, 8 in process)
  • There are over 100 Traffic Signal Maintaining Agencies/TSMA that DO NOT meet the criteria above

    • Clarifications in process:
  • Only 1/3 of signals have been mapped in Jacksonville TSMA 381 (Signals) - TRINT is requesting tabular list from TSMA
  • TRINT is requesting clarifications on 3 Traffic Signals in Lake County
  • TRINT is requesting clarifications on 2 Traffic Signals in City of St. Petersburg
  • TRINT is requesting clarifications on several Traffic Signals that have jurisdiction overlap City of Naples and Collier County
  • TRINT is requesting clarifications on several Traffic Signals that have jurisdiction overlap City of Bradenton and Manatee County
  • TRINT is requesting clarifications on one (SR 39 and Sam Allen Rd) Traffic Signal that have jurisdiction overlap Plant City and Hillsborough County

    • Data not yet obtained:
  • Several TSMA have not been mapped that TRINT believes to have more than 20 interconnected traffic signals. These government agencies or entities have not shared shared data with Transportation Intelligence/TRINT. Trint has requested any list (even handwritten ones), forms. maps, from such TSMAs. Some more advanced agencies provide KML, GIS/Shape file. some less advanced agencies provide a excel file, text file or PDF file. There were also a couple of TSMAs that provided a handwritten list. However there are TSMA that have yet to share traffic signal information even after 30 days (with weekly request)
  • These TSMA are: Citrus County, Putnam County, Lake City, City of Winter Haven, and City of Clearwater
  • Please report any data inaccuracies that you find at data@trint.us
